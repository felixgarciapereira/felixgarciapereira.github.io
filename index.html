About Me

I am a PhD student in climate science at Universidad Complutense de Madrid. I finished my MSc studies in Meteorology and Geophysics in 2019 and worked as a research assistant for the Guadarrama Monitoring Network (GuMNet) until 2020. In this context, I used observational subsurface temperature data to evaluate the underground heat transfer regime at some sites in Central Spain. Nowadays, my research activity is devoted to studying the impact of implementing a more realistic physical representation of land surface models (LSMs) in Earth System Models (ESMs), with a special focus on the impacts of the LSM depth on susbsurface heat transfer. My main scientific question is to address the effects of deepening the LSM zero-flux bottom boundary condition on the long-term ground surface and subsurface temperature variability, land heat uptake, and subsequently the terrestrial energy partioning. For that reason, I have some modeling experience running the Max Planck Institute for Meteorology Earth System Model (MPI-ESM), which participates in the Coupled Model Intercomparison Proyect Phase 6 (CMIP6), and its respective LSM, JSBACH. In this context, I contributed to setting up and monitoring the first ESM past2k simulation with a deep LSM. Furthermore, I explored the combined effects of deepening the LSM with changing the soil hydrology in Arctic soils holding permafrost.


If you are interested in soil climate and modeling, permafrost physics, or land-atmophere interactions, please, reach me out!
